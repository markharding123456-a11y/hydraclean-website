Asbestos Abatement

Safe removal. No shortcuts.

Certified asbestos inspection, abatement, and disposal for residential and commercial properties.

IICRC Certified WorkSafeBC BBB Accredited

The Problem

Asbestos was used extensively in building materials for decades — insulation, floor tiles, pipe wrap, drywall compound, roofing — and can be found in homes of any age, including newer construction. When disturbed, asbestos fibres become airborne and can cause serious lung disease.

Any renovation or demolition project requires an asbestos inspection — regardless of building age. This is not optional — it is a regulatory requirement in British Columbia. WorkSafeBC mandates that suspect materials be tested before any work that could disturb them.

Improper handling of asbestos-containing materials puts workers, occupants, and neighbours at risk. Fines for non-compliance are significant, and the health consequences of exposure can be severe and irreversible.

Our Process

1

Inspection & Sampling

Certified inspectors identify suspect materials throughout your building. Samples are collected and sent to an accredited laboratory for analysis to confirm the presence and type of asbestos.

2

Abatement Plan

A WorkSafeBC-compliant work plan is developed detailing removal procedures, containment methods, and safety protocols. Required notifications are filed with the appropriate authorities.

3

Containment & Removal

Full containment with negative air pressure and HEPA filtration prevents fibre release. Trained workers in protective equipment safely remove asbestos-containing materials following strict protocols.

4

Disposal

Asbestos waste is double-bagged, labelled, and transported by licensed carriers to approved disposal facilities. Complete manifests and documentation are maintained for your records.

5

Air Clearance Testing

Third-party air monitoring confirms that airborne fibre levels are below safe thresholds. Clearance certificates are issued before the area is released for reoccupation or further construction.

What To Do Right Now

Asbestos is only dangerous when fibres become airborne. If suspect materials are intact and undisturbed, they pose minimal risk. Do not drill, cut, sand, scrape, or break any materials you suspect may contain asbestos.
If you have already started work and discover suspect materials, stop immediately. Do not continue until a certified inspector has assessed the materials and confirmed whether asbestos is present.
Close off the area where suspect materials have been disturbed. Limit foot traffic and keep doors closed to prevent potential fibre spread to other parts of the building.
Standard cleaning methods spread asbestos fibres into the air where they can be inhaled. Only HEPA-rated equipment designed for asbestos work should be used. Regular shop vacuums will make the situation significantly worse.
A certified building inspector can identify suspect materials, collect samples for laboratory analysis, and advise on next steps. This is a legal requirement before any renovation or demolition work in BC, regardless of building age.
