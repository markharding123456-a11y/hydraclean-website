Vehicle Impact Damage

Structural damage.
Structural expertise.

Emergency stabilization and complete restoration after vehicle impact to buildings.

The Problem

When a vehicle strikes a building, the damage extends far beyond the impact zone. Structural integrity may be compromised, utilities can be damaged, and the building may be unsafe to occupy.

Immediate stabilization is critical to prevent collapse, followed by engineering assessment and comprehensive restoration.

Our Process

01

Emergency Stabilization

Structural shoring, temporary barriers, utility shut-off coordination. We secure the building to prevent further collapse and protect surrounding areas.

02

Engineering Assessment

Structural engineer evaluation, damage scope documentation. A professional assessment determines the full extent of structural compromise and guides the repair plan.

03

Insurance & Documentation

Complete documentation, liaison with auto and property insurance. Vehicle impacts often involve multiple insurance policies, and we coordinate with all parties.

04

Structural Repair

Remove damaged materials, rebuild to code. All structural repairs are completed to current building code standards with proper permitting.

05

Finishing

Match existing finishes, final inspection, occupancy clearance. The goal is a seamless restoration where the damage is undetectable.

What To Do Right Now

A vehicle impact can compromise the structural integrity of the entire building, not just the impact zone. Get everyone out until the structure has been assessed by a professional engineer.
Emergency services need to assess the scene for injuries, secure the area, and coordinate utility shutoffs. A police report is also essential for insurance claims.
Even if the building appears stable, hidden structural damage can cause delayed collapse. Wait for a structural engineer or emergency services to confirm the building is safe before re-entering.
Damaged gas lines, electrical hazards, and unstable structures all pose risks to bystanders. Maintain a safe perimeter and follow directions from emergency services.
If you can safely take photos from a distance, document the damage from multiple angles. This will be valuable for insurance claims and the engineering assessment.
