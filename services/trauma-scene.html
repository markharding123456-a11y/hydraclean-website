Trauma & Crime Scene Cleanup

Handled with care.

Compassionate, discreet biohazard cleanup and decontamination services.

The Problem

Trauma scenes, unattended deaths, and crime scenes present serious biohazard risks. Blood, bodily fluids, and biological materials require specialized handling, equipment, and disposal procedures.

This work demands not only technical expertise but sensitivity and discretion during an incredibly difficult time.

Our Process

01

Confidential Response

Discreet arrival, assessment of scope and biohazard level. We arrive in unmarked vehicles and work with complete discretion.

02

Containment & Safety

PPE protocols, containment barriers, ventilation. The area is secured to prevent cross-contamination and protect everyone involved.

03

Biohazard Removal

Removal and proper disposal of all biological materials per regulations. Every trace is identified, removed, and disposed of in compliance with health and safety standards.

04

Decontamination

Hospital-grade disinfection, antimicrobial treatment of all surfaces. We ensure no biological hazard remains in the environment.

05

Restoration

Repair or replacement of affected materials, odour elimination, final verification. The space is returned to a safe, livable condition.

What To Do Right Now

Biohazard exposure is dangerous. Blood and bodily fluids can carry bloodborne pathogens including HIV, Hepatitis B, and Hepatitis C. Professional equipment and training are required for safe handling.
Keep everyone out of the affected area until professional cleanup is complete. This protects people from biohazard exposure and prevents cross-contamination to other parts of the property.
If you can safely open windows without entering the affected area, do so. Ventilation helps reduce odour and airborne contaminants while you wait for the professional team to arrive.
If the scene has not been cleared by law enforcement, contact them first. Cleanup cannot begin until the scene has been officially released by the relevant authorities.
This is not something you need to handle alone. Reach out to family, friends, or professional counselling services. We can provide referrals to victim services and crisis support if needed.
